$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{ \frac{I}{mgd} }
$$
Center of mass at $\frac{2H}{3}$
$$
I = \int r^2\,\ dm
$$
$$
\lambda = \frac{dm}{dr}
$$
$$
\lambda\,\ dr = dm
$$
$$
dm = cy\,\ dy
$$
$$
I = \int_{0}^H cy^3\,\ dy = \frac{cH^4}{4}
$$
$$
I = \frac{cH^4}{4} + \frac{4MH^2}{9}
$$
$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{ \frac{\left( \frac{cH^4}{4}+\frac{4MH^2}{9} \right)}{\frac{2MgH}{3}} }
$$
