Half Life
 ^yIaTSjW2

The time required for the concentration of a reactant to decrease by half.
 ^sLO21Qjz

Only concerned with the half life or first order reactions because half-life 
remains constant and independent of concentration. ^E5uIg9xf

Reaction Mechanisms ^o5nmCVr9

Reactants turning into products usually occurs in multiple steps.
Concerned with the process/steps of reactions
 ^5I3bGuKb

• molecularity - number of molecules colliding
    • unimolecular - one reactant molecule
    • bimolecular - two reactant molecules
    • termolecular - three reactant molecules

• Elementary steps (ES) - individual steps that add up to the overall rxn

• Rate-determining step (RDS) - slowest step that limits the overall rxn

• Intermediate - produced in one ES and then consumed in the next

• Catalyst - used to speed up rxn and then reproduced

• Both intermediate and catalyst reactants don't appear in overall rxn

 ^bWW5VJgm

Theoretical Rate Laws ^h20AQamH

• The only time you can use coefficients as orders
    • Use the slow, RDS

• Must still prove the TRL by experimentation

1. To prove a rxn mechanism, ES must add up to the overall rxn
2. TRL must match the experimentally determined rate law

 ^PzfPimLA

RDS ^xjBA6Avo

1. Elementary steps cancel NOBr2 to have the same overall reaction

2. Substitute NOBr2 for NO + BR2 to find theoretical rate law




Theoretical rate law ends up the same as experimental one
 ^PmXSY7tq

• This can be done because the slow step isn't first and previous step
   is a fast, equilibrium step ^WeFQqdhX

Catalysts ^ASTLVZKL

• Homogeneous - catalyst is the same phase as reactants
• Heterogeneous - catalyst is different phase from reactants
• Enzymatic - binds with reactants (better orientation or lower energy)
 ^y0ii8zHi
